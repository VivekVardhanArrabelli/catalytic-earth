# Safety Scope

Catalytic Earth is a discovery and annotation project for beneficial enzyme
function. It should stay focused on interpretation, benchmarking, and candidate
prioritization for legitimate environmental, industrial, and biological research.

## In Scope

- Mechanism-level annotation of enzymes.
- Misannotation detection.
- Public-data knowledge graph construction.
- Candidate ranking for benign chemistry.
- Dossiers for expert review.
- Assay-family suggestions at a high level.
- Review-only external-source transfer manifests that keep computational
  candidates non-countable until label-factory gates and expert evidence clear.

## Out of Scope

- Protocols for engineering harmful biological functions.
- Optimization of virulence, toxicity, immune escape, or resistance.
- Instructions for culturing, modifying, or deploying organisms.
- Operational wet-lab methods.
- Claims that computational predictions are experimentally validated.
- Treating external-source transfer candidates as validated or countable labels
  without explicit review and factory-gate artifacts.

## Candidate Language

Use:

- "hypothesis"
- "candidate"
- "mechanistic rationale"
- "requires wet-lab validation"
- "uncertainty"

Avoid:

- "confirmed"
- "validated"
- "will degrade"
- "works"
- "safe to deploy"

unless the statement is backed by experimental evidence from a cited source.
